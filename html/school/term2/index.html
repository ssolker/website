Term 2

Information about the Employer

ComPeters Inc is a small software company, currently based in Guelph, that creates and supports software systems for the Utility and Mapping Industry. ComPeters also provides consulting for all software related endeavors, software design, configuration and set-up. They are involved in creating custom software to build a company’s automated process and have created a number of software programs like UtiLocate 2 and uExcavate for example.

Goals

Below I have included my reflection goals for the work term.
My goals were directed to improving my performance on the job and my technical skills.

Job Description

During my 4-month work term I had the title of Programmer again and for the most part worked on their uExcavate software. As it was a small company and being a returning hire, they trusted me with a lot of development and design ideas. For the majority of my time there, I was the only employee working on their uExcavate software. The term started out as tending to bugs in uExcavate, but with the addition of new customers came a lot of new development and I got to experience working with the customer to provide them with software to suit their needs.

Some aspects of the job included:

  • Modifying a parser to parse emails
  • Web development/design
  • Writing database queries
  • Creating AWS lambda functions
  • Working with API's

The transferable skills I needed during my work term were communication, time management, adaptability, decision making. I then also needed a lot of technical skills. I worked a lot with AWS, programmed in javascript, php, html, css and java. Most of what I needed to learn, I learnt on the job, but school definitely gave me a good base to work off of. School helped me know what to look for when designing usable software and creating good documentation like UML diagrams.

Academic Relationship

My last coop at ComPeters I worked in mostly VB and Java, however this work term I also programmed in Javascript, PHP, HTML and CSS. Web development was not touched on much in school which is why I believe this work term definitely advanced my academic experience.

Not only did I get to work with programming languages I was unfamiliar with, I was given the opportunity to work with different programming services like AWS lambda and their Cloud 9 IDE. I also got to work with some third-party api’s, which is something I have never done in school and now believe I have a good grasp on how to use api’s in the future.

Conclusions

With my second work term finished, I feel ever more confident that this is the field I want to pursue. As I may have mentioned before, school has helped me create a good base, however I feel like both my transferable and technical skills improve during my work terms.

While there are still many areas where I feel I need to improve on, the more experience I get, the more I understand what it means to be a good co-worker, employee and programmer.
